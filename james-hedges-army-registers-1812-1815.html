James Hedges of Ohio in Army registers, 1812-1815

The federal military career of James Hedges (Mansfield, Richland, Ohio) in the War of 1812, as given in United States Army register lists. James was in the regular army and not in the state militia, though he may have been in the militia after the War of 1812.

Summary

Based on the registers below, the general outline of James' Army career is:

  • 1812 (12 March): First Lieutenant, Second Regiment Light Dragoons. Appointed in Ohio. Dragoons led by Colonel James Burn.
  • 1812 (18 December): 1st Lieutenant, 2nd Light Dragoons. Wounded at Mississienway River Ind.
  • 1814 (21 April): 1st Lieutenant, 2nd Light Dragoons: Harrisburg, Pennsylvania. Promoted to Captain, 26th U.S. Infantry (Burlington, Vermont), led by Colonel Isaac Clark [briefly known as the 48th Infantry]
  • 1814 (26 July) - 1815 (27 March): Captain, 26th Infantry. On Recruiting service in Pennsylvania (Harrisburg & Philadelphia).
  • 1815 (15 June): Captain, 26th Infantry. Honorably discharged.
