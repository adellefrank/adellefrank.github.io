Obituaries of Nathan Zelkind from Lake City (Florida) and Valdosta (Georgia) in 1964

 Obituaries for Nathan Zelkind of Lake City, Florida from 1964.

Citation 1 (21 Feb 1964)

"Nathan Zelkind", obituary, 21 Feb 1964 (Friday), page 3B; Lake City Reporter & Columbia Gazette; Columbia, Florida, United States; microfilmed newspaper at Florida Gateway College Library, Lake City.

Citation 2 (19 Feb 1964)

"Zelkind Rites", funeral notice for Nathan Zelkind, 19 Feb 1964 (Wednesday), page 2; Valdosta Daily Times; Lowndes, Georgia, United States; microfilmed newspaper at Valdosta State University Library.

Citation 3 (17 Feb 1964)

"Nathan Zelkind", obituary, 17 Feb 1964 (Monday), page 3; Valdosta Daily Times; Lowndes, Georgia, United States; microfilmed newspaper at Valdosta State University Library.

Transcript of Feb 21 [paragraphs and lists added]

Nathan Zelkind, 61 [born around 1903], died Monday [17 Feb 1964] morning at Lake Shore Hospital [in Lake City, Columbia, Florida: now called Shands Lake Shore Regional Medical Center] after a lengthy illness.

Mr. Zelkind was born in Russia but came to this country as a young man. He had lived in Lake City for the past 25 years [since about 1939] and operated a dry goods store here.

He is survived by

  • his widow, 
    • the former Ann Lazarus;
  • one daughter, 
    • Miss Rosalind Zelkind, Lake City;
  • and two sisters,
    1. Mrs. Morris Silver and
    2. Miss Jean Zelkind, both of Long Island, N.Y.

Funeral services were held Tuesday afternoon [16. Feb] at the McGowan Funeral Home in Valdosta, Ga [Georgia]. Burial was in Sunset Hill Cemetery [in Valdosta, Georgia].

Active pallbearers, from Valdosta, were:

  • Joe Kalin,
  • Dick Wilson,
  • Lamar Golivesky,
  • Abe Golivesky,
  • Harry Karlip, and
  • Charles Levin of Jasper, Fla [Florida].

Honorary pallbearers,

from Valdosta, were:

  • T. W. Oliver,
  • Fred Nijem,
  • William Pearlman, and
  • Abe Pincus, Quitman, Ga. [Georgia]

Transcript of Feb 19 [emphases added]

Funeral services for Nathan Zelkind of Lake City, Fla., who died Monday [17 Feb], were held Tuesday [18 Feb] at the McGowan Funeral Home.

Rabbi Samuel I. Zakuto conducted the rites. Burial was in Sunset Hill Cemetery [Valdosta].

Transcript of Feb 17 [list and emphases added]

Nathan Zelkind, 55 [born about 1909], of Lake City, Fla., died in Lake Shore Hospital there this morning [17 Feb].

Mr. Zelkind was a merchant in Lake City and had run a dry goods store for 25 years.

He is survived by

  • his wife,
    • the former Ann Lazarus of Valdosta;
  • one daughter,
    • Miss Rosalind Zelkind of Lake City;
  • and two sisters,
    1. Miss Jean Zelkind and
    2. Mrs. Anna Silvers, both of New York City.

Funeral services will be held at 4 p.m. Tuesday [18 Feb] at McGowan's Funeral Home. Burial will be in Sunset Hill Cemetery [Valdosta].
