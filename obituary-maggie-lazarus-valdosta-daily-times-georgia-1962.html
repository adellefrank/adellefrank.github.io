Obituaries of Maggie Lazarus from Valdosta Daily Times (Georgia) in 1962

Obituaries for Maggie (Oppert) Lazarus of Valdosta, Georgia from 1962.

Citation 1 (13 Jun 1962)

"Mrs. Maggie Lazarus", obituary, 13 Jun 1962 (Wednesday), page 11, col. 5; Valdosta Daily Times, newspaper; Lowndes, Georgia, United States; microfilm at Valdosta State University Library.

Citation 2 (14 Jun 1962)

"Mrs. Lazarus rites", funeral notice, 14 Jun 1962 (Thursday), page 4; Valdosta Daily Times, newspaper; Lowndes, Georgia, United States; microfilm at Valdosta State University Library.

Transcript of June 13 [paragraphs and lists added]

Mrs. Maggie Lazarus, 84 [born about 1878], a long-time resident of Valdosta, died in Pineview General Hospital [in Valdosta, Georgia, now called South Georgia Medical Center] Tuesday [12 June] after a lengthy illness.

Survivors include: 

  • Three sons, 
    1. Louis Lazarus, West Palm Beach, Fla., 
    2. Sam Lazarus, Brewton, Ala.,
    3. William Lazarus, Trenton, Fla.;
  • five daughters, 
    1. Mrs. Ethel Hopkins, Valdosta,
    2. Miss Rose Lazarus, Valdosta,
    3. Mrs. Lucile Taylor, Quitman [Georgia],
    4. Mrs. Myrtle Waters, Quitman, and
    5. Mrs. Ann Zelkind, Lake City, Fla.,
  • one sister, 
    1. Mrs. Ella Tew, Headland, Ala.;
  • seven grandchildren and
  • five great-grandchildren.

Funeral services were to be held today [13 Jun] at 2 p.m. at the McGowan Funderal Home [308 N. Patterson Street, Valdosta]. Burial is to follow in Sunset Hill Cemetery [Valdosta].

Mrs. Lazarus a resident of Valdosta for the past 54 years [since about 1908], was a member of the Valdosta Hebrew Congregation [later known as Temple Israel].

Transcript of June 14 [paragraphs and lists added]

Funeral services for Mrs. Maggie Lazarus, who died Tuesday [12 June], were held at 2 p. m. Wednesday [13 June] in the chapel of McGowan funeral home [308 N. Patterson Street, Valdosta].

Rabbi Samuel Zakuto of Temple Israel in Valdosta conducted the services.

Honorary pallbearers were 

  • T. W. Oliver,
  • Robert Kilgore,
  • William Landey,
  • Harry Abrahams,
  • Abe Golivesky,
  • Dewey [Horn?],
  • Sol Miller,
  • Abe Pincus,
  • W. J. Hewett,
  • Mendel Lazarus,
  • Gordon Williams,
  • Leland Jordan, and
  • R. A. Lester.

Active pallbearers were

  • Jake Lazarus,
  • Henry Taylor,
  • Leon Lazarus,
  • William Lazarus,
  • Jack Lazarus, and
  • Nat Polonsky.

Burial was in the Hebrew section of Sunset Hill [Valdosta cemetery].
